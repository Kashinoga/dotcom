# How do I vote?

# tl;dr

1. Are you registered to vote in your state of residency?
    <details>
        <summary>Iowa</summary>
        <p>
            Check your registration status and register at: <a href="https://sos.iowa.gov/elections/voterinformation/voterregistration.html">Iowa Secretary of State: Voter Registration</a>
        </p>
    </details>
2. Is it time to vote?
<details>
    <summary>Iowa</summary>
    <p>
    <b>2022 PRIMARY ELECTION IMPORTANT DATES</b> <br />
    Tuesday, March 29 – First Day to File Absentee Ballot Request with County Auditor <br />
    Wednesday, May 18 – First Day of Absentee Voting <br />
    Monday, May 23 – Pre-registration Deadline; Deadline to Request Absentee Ballot to be Mailed <br />
    Saturday, June 4 – Auditors’ Offices Open for Absentee Voting <br />
    Monday, June 6 – Absentee Ballot In-Person Deadline <br />
    Tuesday, June 7 – Primary Election Day; Absentee Ballots Receipt Deadline <br />
    </p>
    <p>
    <b>2022 GENERAL ELECTION IMPORTANT DATES</b> <br />
    Tuesday, August 30 – First Day to File Absentee Ballot Request with County Auditor <br />
    Wednesday, October 19 – First Day of Absentee Voting <br />
    Monday, October 24 – Pre-registration Deadline; Deadline to Request Absentee Ballot to be Mailed <br />
    Saturday, November 5 – Auditors’ Offices Open for Absentee Voting <br />
    Monday, November 7 – Absentee Ballot In-Person Deadline <br />
    Tuesday, November 8 – General Election Day; Absentee Ballots Receipt Deadline <br />
    Polls are open from 7:00 a.m. until 8:00 p.m. CST. Absentee ballot must be received in your county auditor’s office prior to 8:00 PM CST on Election Day. <br />
    </p>
</details> 
3. Where do I vote?
    <details>
        <summary>Iowa</summary>
        <b>In Person, Mail</b>
            <p>
                Wherever! Just make sure that you can send and receive snail mail... See the next step on how to vote by mail.
            </p>
        <b>In Person, Absentee</b>
        <p>
            In-person absentee voting for the June 7 Primary Election is available Wednesday, May 18 – Monday, June 6. You can vote in your county auditor’s office or at an appropriate satellite location, if available.
        </p>
        <b>In Person, Election Day</b>
        <p>
            Iowa law requires each voter to vote in the polling place for the precinct where the voter lives. Voters with questions about where to vote may contact the <a href="https://sos.iowa.gov/elections/auditors/auditorslist.html">county auditor</a> or <a href="https://sos.iowa.gov/elections/voterreg/pollingplace/search.aspx">find their precinct online</a>.
        </p>
    </details>
4. How do I vote?
    <details>
        <summary>Iowa</summary>
        <b>In Person, Mail</b>
            <p>
                <ul>
                    <li>1. Download and fill out <a href="https://sos.iowa.gov/elections/pdf/absenteeballotapp.pdf">the request form</a></li>
                    <li>2. Send in your completed request form to <a href="https://sos.iowa.gov/elections/auditors/auditorslist.html">your county auditor</a></li>
                    <li>3. Receive your ballot from your county auditor</li>
                    <li>4. Fill in your ballot</li>
                    <li>5. Return your ballot to your county auditor (either by mail or in person)</li>
                </ul>
            </p>
        <b>In Person, Absentee</b>
        <p>
            In-person absentee voting for the June 7 Primary Election is available Wednesday, May 18 – Monday, June 6. You can vote in your county auditor’s office or at an appropriate satellite location, if available.
        </p>
        <p>
            Make sure you bring in an appropriate ID as if you were voting in person on election day.
        </p>
        <b>In Person, Election Day</b>
        <p>
            <ul>
                <li>1. Grab your ID</li>
                    <ul>
                        <li>1. Driver's License</li>
                        <li>2. Non-Operator ID</li>
                        <li>3. Voter Identification Card</li>
                        <li>4. U.S. Passport</li>
                        <li>5. U.S. Veteran or Military ID</li>
                        <li>6. Tribal Card or Document</li>
                    </ul>
                <li>2. Go to your polling place</li>
                <li>3. Vote!</li>
                    <ul>
                        <li>1. Volunteers at your polling place will be able to guide you through the process</li>
                    </ul>
            </ul>
        </p>
    </details>
